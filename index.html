NTU FINTECH HACKATHON · 2016

Price it down
like a control system.

A grocery item loses value as it nears expiry. The classic fix — a last-minute "reduced to clear" sticker — wastes 40–60% of stock. This project treats markdown pricing as a PID control loop that eases the price down continuously, clearing inventory right as it expires.

▶ Open Interactive Simulation 📖 Documentation ⌥ Source
u(t) = Kp·e(t)  +  Ki·∫e(t)dt  +  Kd·de/dt     # e(t) = inventory − ideal sell-down
How it works
Three terms, one smooth glide-down.
Kp · e(t)

Proportional

Reacts to today's excess stock — too much left on the shelf pushes the price down now.

Ki · ∫e dt

Integral

Chases away persistent over-stock that the proportional term alone can't clear.

Kd · de/dt

Derivative

Damps over-discounting as inventory starts dropping fast near the deadline.

Auto-tune ✦

Pattern search

~1000 Monte-Carlo evals find gains that maximise welfare minus price volatility — per product.
