PID Perishable Pricing

NTU Fintech Hackathon 2016. A grocery item is stocked with a fixed shelf life and loses value as it nears expiry — linearly for produce and dairy, exponentially for meat and seafood (from the willingness-to-pay-for-freshness literature). Classical pricing holds the sticker flat and marks down once, late — so much of the stock spoils. A PID controller eases the price down continuously to track a convex ideal sell-down (sell faster while fresh), clearing inventory right by expiry and balancing customer happiness against shop happiness. Every season is replayed many times; the shaded bands show the P10–P90 uncertainty. Hit Auto-tune ✦ to search the gain space for the Kp/Ki/Kd that maximise net welfare with smooth, stable pricing.
